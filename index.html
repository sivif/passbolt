🔍 Introduction to Password Managers
  • What is a Traditional Password Manager? A digital vault solution designed primarily for individual or centralized credential storage.
  • Core Functionality: Acts as a locked repository for storing web logins, secure notes, and sensitive credentials.
  • Standard Workflow: Users authenticate with a single Master Password to unlock a personal database usually managed by a third-party cloud.

🔍 FAQs of Traditional Password Managers
Q: Where is data stored?
A: Most mainstream options rely on centralized corporate cloud servers or local standalone databases.
Q: Can the service provider reset my password?
A: In many traditional setups, yes, through standard email loops or administrator account overrides.
Q: Does it support safe data-sharing options?
A: Sharing is usually limited to broad folder-level configurations or basic syncing, often lacking isolated, per-user cryptographic keys.

🔍 Introduction to Passbolt / Use cases
  • What is Passbolt? An open-source, team-first, zero-knowledge password manager designed for technical and enterprise environments.
  • The Two-Gate Security Model: Utilizes local asymmetric OpenPGP keys. Your Master Password unlocks your unique local Private Key right inside your browser extension to unscramble your passwords. The actual server never sees your master password or unencrypted secrets.
  • Key Use Cases:
    • Data Sovereignty: Organizations looking to self-host their own vault so corporate logins never touch a third-party server.
    • Granular Vault Management: Sharing access to a single password with specific team members without giving away control of an entire folder.
    • Securing Infrastructure: Storing individual API keys, server SSH credentials, or config profiles away from shared chats.

🔍 Passbolt FAQs & Emergency Scenarios
⚠ What if I lose my Private Key or forget my Master Password?
Because Passbolt is built on asymmetric OpenPGP encryption, your key pair is strictly your responsibility. Systems administrators do not store, manage, or have access to your Private Key or Master Password. If lost, your account data cannot be recovered. Always download and secure your Recovery Kit.
Q: What if the Passbolt server gets hacked?
A: Attackers only compromise encrypted blobs. Because decryption requires your local Private Key and Master Password, a server breach does not expose your passwords.
Q: What if the server goes down or is blocked on Wi-Fi?
A: Critical Limitation: Passbolt is a strict client-server application and lacks an offline mode. If the extension session has expired or cannot reach the server, it will freeze on "Connecting..." and completely lock you out.
Q: Can IT admins or Passbolt see my secrets?
A: No. Your browser extension encrypts data locally into gibberish before sending it anywhere. Only your device holds the decryption mechanics.
Q: Why force a browser extension?
A: Web apps can be tampered with. A local extension performs all critical cryptographic handshakes locally, protecting you from phishing vectors.
